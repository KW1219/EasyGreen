The problem

01 — context

As students reach high school, more of them start paying closer attention to how they look — weight, physique, appearance. Diet sits underneath almost all of it. Some people are trying to eat more, some less, and once specific nutrients enter the picture, it gets complicated fast. Social media doesn't help: it's an easy place to pick up confident-sounding advice that's actually wrong.

01 Appearance pressureGrowing focus on beauty, weight, and physique among peers Rising
02 Conflicting goalsSome aiming to eat more, others less — often without a clear plan Common
03 Nutrient complexityCarbs, sugar, protein, dairy — hard to weigh against each other Confusing
04 Social media misinformationConfident advice online that isn't backed by evidence Unverified
05 Long-term riskUnwanted physiques or harmful diets when bad advice sticks High stakes

The solution

One plan, built from your actual goals — not a stranger's.

PlateScore takes your goals, restrictions, and health information and turns them into a schedule built for you. Every suggestion comes with a confidence score, a short list of reasons, and links to the research behind it — so it's easy to follow and easy to trust.
